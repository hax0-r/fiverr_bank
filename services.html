The Benefits of Using Online Banking

Online banks are gaining more and more attention due to their many advantages over traditional banking institutions, offering an innovative approach to managing your finances. The growing popularity of digital banks is mainly based on two key aspects: banking fees and ease of use.

Bank fees are virtually non-existent and always transparent, eliminating any unpleasant surprises. All banking operations such as transfers, direct debits, withdrawals, and others are completely free in France and the Eurozone. In addition, the bank card and checkbooks are provided free of charge, along with additional benefits such as travel insurance or partner promotions.
Managing your account, budget and expenses is made much easier with innovative and user-friendly mobile applications. This gives you complete control over your finances and banking products such as loans or savings accounts, which you can apply for online in just a few moments. In addition, all transactions, banking operations and budget forecasts can be carried out in real time from your phone, tablet or computer. ​​



The criteria for selecting a good online bank

While online banks have many advantages over traditional banks, they differ in terms of innovation, cost-free nature, and control over your bank account. There are a wide variety of online banks, with some standing out from the rest. While most offer the same banking products, the difference lies in five main aspects:

Mobile App: As the primary means of managing and communicating with the bank, the app is the most crucial feature of an online bank. The choice depends on how useful you plan to use it and your needs in terms of banking products and customer service.

Rates and fees: The absence of bank fees on major transactions is a major advantage of online banks. Some establishments include more transactions in their free offers, while others offer "all-inclusive" annual packages.

Banking products: Although all online banks offer the same banking products, each may present specific advantages on a particular product, whether it be credits, insurance, financial investments or savings accounts.

Branch Presence: With the advent of e-checks and instant transfers via apps or SMS, the need for physical branches has decreased significantly. However, for some professionals who require frequent deposits of checks or cash, the proximity of a branch may be essential.

Bank Cards: Bank card offerings vary from one online bank to another. Some offer a single offer, while others offer a wide range depending on your income, each with its own benefits such as travel insurance or concierge services.
